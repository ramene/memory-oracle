## Reversal protocol (for emergencies, surgery, major bleed)

In the event of acute major hemorrhage, intracranial bleed, or emergency surgical intervention, anticoagulation must be reversed immediately:

1. **Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)** — 10–15 mL/kg IV (typically 2–4 units)
2. **Vitamin K (phytonadione)** — 10 mg IV slow infusion over 30 min
3. **4-factor Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (4F-PCC)** if FFP contraindicated — 25–50 units/kg IV
4. Recheck INR at 30 min and 4 hr post-reversal

**Hold** all anticoagulant doses for minimum 5 days prior to elective surgery. Bridge with LMWH if thromboembolic risk warrants (per CHA₂DS₂-VASc + bleeding risk assessment).

## Drug interactions to flag

- Amoxicillin, ciprofloxacin, fluconazole, metronidazole → INR amplification, dose reduction warranted
- NSAIDs — bleeding risk, avoid unless GI prophylaxis
- Cranberry juice, leafy greens — counsel patient on consistency
